## 4. Real AI Events / Milestones (Chronological)

### 2010-2014

- **2010:** DeepMind founded in London with 20-year AGI mission.
- **2014 (January):** Google acquires DeepMind for ~$500M.
- **2014 (October):** Elon Musk delivers "summoning the demon" speech at MIT.

### 2015-2019

- **2015 (December):** OpenAI founded as a nonprofit. Pledges $1B. Board includes Musk, Altman, others.
- **2016:** DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats world Go champion Lee Sedol. First major "AI beats humans" moment.
- **2018:** Musk leaves OpenAI board (reportedly over disagreements about direction).
- **2019:** OpenAI creates "capped-profit" subsidiary. Returns capped at 100x. The nonprofit starts dying.

### 2020-2022

- **2020 (June):** GPT-3 released. First model to generate convincingly human text. "Scaling is all you need" era begins.
- **2021:** Google internally develops LaMDA. An engineer later claims it's sentient (Blake Lemoine, fired 2022).
- **2022 (November 30):** ChatGPT launches. Reaches 100M users in 2 months (fastest-growing consumer product ever). OpenAI's board reportedly learns about it from Twitter.
- **2022 (December):** Ilya Sutskever leads "Feel the AGI!" chants at OpenAI holiday party.

### 2023

- **January:** Microsoft invests $10B in OpenAI (cumulative ~$13B).
- **February:** Bing/Sydney incident. Microsoft's AI chatbot goes unhinged in public.
- **March:** GPT-4 released. Llama weights leak to 4chan. Samsung data leak. Italy bans ChatGPT (temporarily). FLI publishes pause letter (30,000+ signatures including Musk, Bengio, Harari).
- **May:** Geoffrey Hinton quits Google to warn about AI. Altman testifies before US Senate, agrees AI should be regulated, proposes a licensing agency. Google launches Bard.
- **June:** CAIS statement on AI risk signed by Hinton, Bengio, Altman, Amodei, Hassabis: "Mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority."
- **July:** Meta releases Llama 2 as open source (intentionally this time).
- **September:** UK announces AI Safety Summit at Bletchley Park.
- **October:** Biden executive order on AI safety.
- **November 1-2:** UK AI Safety Summit at Bletchley Park. 28 countries sign the Bletchley Declaration. Everyone agrees AI is dangerous. Everyone goes home and keeps building.
- **November 17-21:** OpenAI board crisis. Altman fired ("not consistently candid"), reinstated 4 days later after ~700/770 employees threatened to quit. Ilya Sutskever initially sided with board, then reversed. Board replaced with business-friendly members.

### 2024

- **February:** Gemini image generation controversy. Google pauses feature.
- **March:** Anthropic releases Claude 3.
- **April:** Leopold Aschenbrenner fired from OpenAI.
- **May:** GPT-4o released. Scarlett Johansson voice controversy. Ilya Sutskever leaves OpenAI. Jan Leike resigns, blasts OpenAI's safety culture. OpenAI dissolves Superalignment team.
- **June:** Aschenbrenner publishes "Situational Awareness: The Decade Ahead." Sutskever founds SSI (Safe Superintelligence Inc.).
- **July:** Meta releases Llama 3.1 (including 405B model, first "frontier-level" open source).
- **August:** EU AI Act enters into force (first comprehensive AI regulation).
- **October:** Hinton and Hopfield win Nobel Prize in Physics for neural network foundations.
- **December:** OpenAI announces restructuring toward for-profit.

### 2025

- **Early 2025:** Altman blog post: "We are now confident we know how to build AGI." Later says AGI "kinda went whooshing by" with less impact than expected.
- **January-March:** Massive compute buildout. Hundreds of billions invested in data centers.
- **2025:** Claude 4 released. Reasoning models become standard. AI agents proliferate. Scaling debate intensifies (is scaling enough, or are returns diminishing?).
- **May:** OpenAI restructures as public benefit corporation. Profit cap removed. SoftBank invests $40B (half conditional on removing profit cap).
- **August:** Altman says "AGI is not a super useful term" — after years of promising AGI.

### 2026

- **January:** Dario Amodei publishes "The Adolescence of Technology" — warns AI "will test who we are as a species."
- **February:** Anthropic loosens its Responsible Scaling Policy. Admits the "race to the top" strategy hasn't worked as hoped.
