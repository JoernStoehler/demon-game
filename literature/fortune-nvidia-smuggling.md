# Four accused in black-market scheme to smuggle hundreds of Nvidia GPUs to China—while raking in millions

- **Author:** Amanda Gerut, News Editor, West Coast
- **Source:** Fortune
- **Published:** November 20, 2025, 7:34 PM ET
- **URL:** https://fortune.com/2025/11/20/nvidia-chips-china-smuggle-ai/

---

Federal authorities arrested four men this week for allegedly shipping contraband Nvidia chips through an elaborate smuggling network. The operation reportedly routed the graphics processing units from Alabama through Malaysia and Thailand to recipients in China, according to a Department of Justice announcement Thursday.

The accused failed to obtain required export permits while attempting to circumvent controls that restrict shipping advanced Nvidia GPUs. According to an indictment, China's military sought the chips for "weapons design and testing, including for weapons of mass destruction as well as in connection with the PRC's development and deployment of advanced AI surveillance tools." The document notes China aims to become "the world leader in AI by 2030."

Export controls on advanced computing chips have been in effect since October 2022, administered by the Department of Commerce's Bureau of Industry and Security.

"As demonstrated by this indictment, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Middle District of Florida is firmly committed to safeguarding our country's national security," stated U.S. Attorney Gregory W. Kehoe in a statement.

**The accused:**

- Hon Ning Ho (Matthew Ho), 34, U.S. citizen born in Hong Kong, Tampa, Florida
- Brian Curtis Raymond, 46, U.S. citizen, Huntsville, Alabama
- Cham Li (Tony Li), 38, from China, San Leandro, California
- Jing Chen, 45, Chinese national on F-1 student visa, Tampa, Florida

The indictment describes Raymond as CEO and sole owner of an unnamed technology distributor licensed to sell Nvidia GPUs. According to LinkedIn, Raymond operates Bitworks, described as delivering "AI at scale with Nvidia HGX and DGX as a certified cloud partner." He also identified himself as chief technology officer of Corvex, an Arlington, Virginia-based AI cloud company, beginning in October.

Corvex stated: "Corvex had no part in the activities cited in the Department of Justice's indictment. The person in question is not an employee of Corvex."

The scheme centered on Janford Realtor LLC, a Tampa-based front company owned and controlled by Ho and Li. The operation successfully smuggled 400 Nvidia A100 GPUs to China between October 2024 and January 2025. Two additional shipments—containing 10 Hewlett Packard Enterprise supercomputers with Nvidia H100 GPUs and 50 Nvidia H200 GPUs—were disrupted by law enforcement.

Each defendant faces a maximum sentence of 20 years on export evasion charges, 10 years on smuggling counts, and 20 years on each money laundering charge.

The arrests coincide with Nvidia's declining China business due to export restrictions. During third-quarter earnings calls Wednesday, Nvidia CFO Colette Kress noted that "purchase orders [have dried] up due to geopolitical issues" while advocating for market access.
