# A. Q. Khan Nuclear Chronology

- **Author:** Michael Laufer
- **Source:** Carnegie Endowment for International Peace
- **Published:** Sep 7, 2005
- **URL:** https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2005/09/a-q-khan-nuclear-chronology

## Khan Builds His Base in Pakistan

*Pre-1985: Khan's early exposure to European technology and supply chains allow him to establish and develop uranium enrichment technologies in Pakistan.*

**1936**
Khan is born in Bhopal, British India. He immigrates to Pakistan in 1952 after partition.

**1961**
Khan moves to Europe to complete studies in West Berlin and later at Technical University in Delft, Holland, receiving a degree in metallurgical engineering in 1967.

**1972**
Khan receives Ph.D. in metallurgical engineering from Catholic University of Leuven in Belgium. In May, he begins work at Physical Dynamic Research Laboratory (FDO), a subcontractor of Ultra Centrifuge Nederland (UCN), the Dutch partner in the Urenco uranium enrichment consortium. Within one week of starting, Khan visits the advanced UCN enrichment facility in Almelo, Netherlands, becoming familiar with Urenco centrifuge operations without official clearance.

**Early 1970s**
Dutch intelligence begins monitoring Khan due to inquiries about technical information unrelated to his own projects.

**1974**
May 18: India conducts its first nuclear test. September: Khan writes to Prime Minister Zulfikar Ali Bhutto offering his services. Late in the year: Khan receives translations of German-designed G-1 and G-2 centrifuges, with unsupervised access for 16 days.

**Late 1970s and Early 1980s**
American intelligence officials convince Dutch authorities on two occasions not to arrest Khan to monitor his activities further.

**1975**
August: Pakistan begins buying uranium enrichment components from European Urenco suppliers. S.A. Butt, a Pakistani embassy physicist in Belgium, contacts a Dutch company for high frequency inverters used to control centrifuge motors. October: Khan is transferred away from enrichment work as Dutch authorities become concerned. He is observed asking "suspicious questions" at a nuclear trade show in Switzerland. December 15: Khan suddenly leaves FDO for Pakistan with copied centrifuge blueprints and contact information for nearly 100 component suppliers.

**1976**
Khan begins centrifuge work with Pakistan Atomic Energy Commission (PAEC). July: Prime Minister Bhutto gives Khan autonomous control over uranium enrichment programs. Khan founds Engineering Research Laboratory (ERL) on July 31, focusing on indigenous uranium enrichment capability.

**1978**
ERL develops working P-1 centrifuge prototypes adapted from the German G-1 design. Pakistan enriches uranium for the first time on April 4 at Kahuta.

**1979**
April: Pakistan is cut off from U.S. economic and military assistance after intelligence learns of the Kahuta enrichment facility. However, strategic importance after the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan prevents meaningful sanctions.

**Early 1980s**
Khan acquires blueprints for the Chinese bomb tested in China's 1966 nuclear explosion. He is reportedly approached by an unknown Arab country requesting nuclear assistance.

**1981**
May 1: ERL is renamed A.Q. Khan Research Laboratories (KRL) by President Zia ul-Haq.

**1983**
Khan is convicted in absentia in Dutch court for nuclear espionage, sentenced to four years imprisonment.

**1985**
Khan's conviction is overturned based on an appeal regarding improper summons service. Dutch prosecution does not renew charges due to inability to serve Khan and obtain documents.

## The Network Flows Both Ways

*Mid-1980s to mid-1990s: Khan develops P-2 centrifuge technology and begins exporting through the same channels used for imports.*

**Mid 1980s**
Pakistan produces enough highly enriched uranium for a nuclear weapon. Khan reportedly begins developing his export network, ordering twice the necessary components. Western intelligence misses this transition from importer to exporter.

**1986 to 1987**
Pakistan and Iran are suspected of signing a secret nuclear cooperation agreement. Iranians may receive centrifuge training at KRL. Khan is suspected of visiting the Iranian Bushehr reactor in February 1986 and January 1987.

**Late 1980s**
Khan and his network reportedly begin nuclear transfers to Iran, continuing through 1995 with P-2 centrifuge components. The Pakistani government claims no transfers occurred after P-1 shipments from 1989 to 1991. German intelligence investigates Pakistani assistance to Iraq, possibly Iran and North Korea, regarding uranium melting processes.

**1987**
Khan is suspected of offering Iran a nuclear technology package including uranium casting assistance, reportedly priced from tens to hundreds of millions of dollars. Khan is believed to make a centrifuge deal with Iran for a 50,000 P-1 centrifuge cascade. KRL begins publishing technical papers outlining advanced design features, including specifications for maraging steel centrifuges and groove etching techniques.

**1988**
Iranian scientists are suspected of receiving nuclear training in Pakistan.

**1989**
Iran is suspected of receiving its first centrifuge assemblies around this time, likely older P-1 components Khan no longer needs. Through 1995, Khan reportedly ships over 2,000 P-1 and P-2 components and sub-assemblies to Iran.

**1990**
An Iraqi memo found during 1995 inspections indicates Khan may have offered nuclear assistance to Iraq in late 1990, including a bomb design and Western European material support. Iraq is believed to have declined, suspecting a sting operation.

**1992**
Pakistan begins missile cooperation with North Korea. KRL becomes responsible for missile research and develops the Ghauri missile with North Korean assistance, potentially establishing connections for nuclear technology transfer.

**1994 or 1995**
More advanced P-2 centrifuge components are suspected to arrive in Iran. B.S.A. Tahir tells Malaysian police that Iran paid approximately $3 million for these parts.

## The Network Expands

*Mid-1990s to Present: Khan expands his network to Libya and North Korea.*

**Mid 1990s**
Khan travels to North Korea at least 13 times before his 2004 public confession, receiving technical assistance for the Ghauri missile. He is suspected of arranging a barter deal exchanging nuclear and missile technologies. Khan travels with military KRL personnel, suggesting possible Pakistani government awareness. Khan is suspected of meeting a top Syrian official in Beirut to offer centrifuge enrichment facility assistance.

**1996**
Pakistan's currency reserve crisis may motivate Khan to expand his nuclear network with North Korea sales. The barter agreement may have helped Pakistan avoid defaulting on external debt.

**1997**
Khan begins transferring centrifuges and components to Libya, providing 20 assembled P-1 centrifuges and components for 200 additional units. Khan's network continues supplying centrifuge components until late 2003. Khan is suspected of beginning nuclear transfers to North Korea around this time, though dates remain uncertain. Transfers continue through 2003, though Pakistan claims they ceased in 2001. Khan may have supplied North Korea with discarded centrifuge and enrichment machines, technical drawings, and depleted uranium hexafluoride. December: Several reports state Pakistan's then-Chief of Army Staff General Jehangir Karamat secretly visited Pyongyang. Khan claims Karamat knew of the Pakistan-North Korea enrichment-for-missiles deal, but Karamat denies visiting North Korea or knowing of proliferation activity.

**1998**
India detonates five nuclear devices on May 11 and 13. Pakistan responds with six nuclear tests on May 28 and 30.

**1999**
Pakistani government releases advertisement of nuclear equipment export procedures, listing gas centrifuges and magnet baffles. Other KRL advertisements reportedly include mushroom cloud drawings and vacuum devices for centrifuge casings.

**2000**
June: Peter Griffin establishes Gulf Technical Industries in Dubai as a Khan network front company. B.S.A. Tahir uses it to order centrifuge components from Malaysia. September: Libya receives two P-2 centrifuges as demonstrator models and orders components for 10,000 more, requiring approximately 1 million total parts.

**2001**
Libya obtains 1.87 tons of uranium hexafluoride for enrichment centrifuge feeding. The source remains uncertain, though 2004 evidence suggests North Korea might have supplied it. March: Khan is forced into retirement, refusing a compensatory "advisor" position. President Musharraf admits Khan's suspected proliferation activity was critical to his removal. Summer: American spy satellites detect missile components being loaded into a Pakistani cargo plane outside Pyongyang. December: B.S.A. Tahir signs a $13 million contract with Scuomi Precision Engineering (SCOPE) in Malaysia for 25,000 aluminum centrifuge components, to be shipped through Dubai front companies.

**Late 2001 or Early 2002**
Libya receives nuclear weapons blueprints of reported Chinese origin with Chinese notes, including a note suggesting "Munir's bomb would be bigger," possibly referencing PAEC's Munir Ahmad Khan.

**2002**
December: Shipments begin from SCOPE of aluminum centrifuge components. Four shipments are believed sent from Malaysia to Dubai before August 2003, en route to Libya.

**2003**
Spring: The State Department announces sanctions against KRL for illegal missile transactions, stating insufficient evidence for nuclear transaction sanctions. April: German authorities intercept a ship in the Suez Canal with aluminum tubing specifications suggesting P-2 centrifuge outer casings destined for North Korea. October: The German cargo ship BBC China is intercepted en route to Libya with components for 1,000 centrifuges manufactured in Malaysia and shipped through Dubai. December: Libya renounces its nuclear weapons program and begins full disclosure to the IAEA.

**2004**
February 4: Khan makes a public television confession of illegal nuclear dealings, claiming he initiated transfers citing "error of judgment." He is pardoned by President Musharraf and placed under house arrest. The Pakistani government claims Khan acted independently without state knowledge. March: A BBC China container arrives in Libya with additional P-2 centrifuge components. Colonel Qaddafi reports the arrival to American intelligence and the IAEA, warning that not all components from Libya's orders had arrived.
