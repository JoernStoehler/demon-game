## UN Panel of Experts — North Korea (Final Report)

**Title:** Report of the Panel of Experts established pursuant to resolution 2680 (2023)
**Document Number:** S/2024/215
**Date:** March 7, 2024
**Pages:** 615
**URL:** https://documents.un.org/doc/undoc/gen/n24/032/68/pdf/n2403268.pdf
**Status:** Public UN document. The Panel was dissolved in April 2024 after Russia vetoed its mandate renewal. This is the final report — no 2025 edition exists.
**Note:** PDF too large for web extraction. Download directly from UN.
**Key content:** Detailed mechanisms of DPRK sanctions evasion including weapons procurement, cyber operations, maritime smuggling, and financial networks.

**Successor body:** The Multilateral Sanctions Monitoring Team (MSMT) was established in February 2025 by 11 nations. Reports available at https://msmt.info and via US State Department.

## Bryan Early — Busted Sanctions

**Title:** Busted Sanctions: Explaining Why Economic Sanctions Fail
**Author:** Bryan R. Early
**Publisher:** Stanford University Press
**Date:** 2015
**ISBN:** 978-0804794138
**URL:** https://www.sup.org/books/politics/busted-sanctions
**Status:** Published book, not freely available online. Core academic work on sanctions evasion.
**Key thesis:** Third-party "sanctions busters" are the primary cause of sanctions failure — both profit-motivated trade busters and politically motivated aid busters. Analysis covers 60+ years of U.S. sanctions.

**Related open-access paper:**
- "Does Punishing Sanctions Busters Work? Sanctions Enforcement and U.S. Trade with Sanctioned States" — Bryan R. Early & Timothy M. Peterson, Political Research Quarterly, 2022. https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/10659129211025620 (abstract only without institutional access)

## USCC — China's Facilitation of Sanctions and Export Control Evasion

**Title:** China's Facilitation of Sanctions and Export Control Evasion
**Publisher:** U.S.-China Economic and Security Review Commission
**Date:** November 14, 2025
**URL:** https://www.uscc.gov/research/chinas-facilitation-sanctions-and-export-control-evasion
**Status:** Full report available as PDF download from USCC website. Web page shows summary only.
**Key content:** China as "the decisive enabler of sanctions evasion" for authoritarian states. Shadow fleets, RMB clearing, cryptocurrency, money laundering. Hong Kong as evasion hub. Alternative payment networks bypassing U.S. financial system.
