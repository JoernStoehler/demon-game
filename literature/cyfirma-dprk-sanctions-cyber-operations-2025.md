# DPRK Sanctions Violations in Cyber Operations Post UN Panel Demise

**Publisher:** CYFIRMA
**Date:** 2025
**URL:** https://www.cyfirma.com/research/dprk-sanctions-violations-in-cyber-operations-post-un-panel-demise/

---

## Executive Summary

The report documents how North Korea intensified illicit cyber activities following the UN Panel of Experts' termination in April 2024. Key findings indicate that "2024 was a record year for North Korean cybercrime, with hackers stealing $1.34 billion across 47 incidents."

## Financial Scale and Trends

**2024 Performance:**
- North Korean actors captured 61% of global cryptocurrency theft
- Represented 102.88% increase compared to 2023
- Attacks exceeding $50-100 million became commonplace

**2025 Trajectory:**
First half of 2025 surpassed prior records, with North Korean actors responsible for approximately $1.5 billion of the $2 billion total stolen.

## Primary Evasion Mechanisms

**Cryptocurrency Laundering Strategy:**
The report describes a "flood the zone" approach where "stolen assets to Bitcoin and other virtual assets, dispersing them across thousands of addresses." Within 48 hours of the Bybit heist, at least $160 million was laundered using cross-chain bridges and high-volume transfers.

**IT Worker Infiltration:**
Thousands of operatives work under false identities globally. They use:
- Stolen U.S. identities and AI-generated deepfake photos
- VPNs and proxy services to obscure locations
- "Laptop farm" infrastructure for remote access
- Fraudulent credentials on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Upwork

## Major Cyber Operations (Post-April 2024)

**Bybit Hack (February 2025):**
"Largest cryptocurrency theft in history" - approximately $1.5 billion in Ethereum stolen. FBI attributed activity to "TraderTraitor" operatives linked to Lazarus Group.

**DMM Bitcoin (May 2024):**
Attackers stole 4,502.9 Bitcoin valued at $305 million, with infiltrators posing as LinkedIn recruiters.

**WazirX Exchange (July 2024):**
North Korean actors stole $235 million from India's largest cryptocurrency exchange.

**KnowBe4 Infiltration (July 2024):**
A North Korean operative "successfully posed as a Principal Software Engineer" using stolen identity and deepfake photo. Malware detected within 25 minutes.

## Defense Technology Targeting

The report emphasizes systematic efforts to acquire military technologies including "nuclear, aerospace, naval, and advanced manufacturing." February 2025 espionage campaigns targeted South Korean entities, while April 2025 operations expanded to European defense organizations.

## Geopolitical Context

**Russia-North Korea Alliance:**
The June 2024 defense pact enables:
- Infrastructure sharing for attack routing
- IT worker recruitment and deployment
- Cryptocurrency laundering networks

The report notes Russia released "millions of dollars in previously frozen North Korean assets" while receiving ballistic missiles and troops for Ukraine operations.

## International Response Mechanisms

**Multilateral Sanctions Monitoring Team (MSMT):**
Formed February 2025 with 11 nations (Australia, Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, Netherlands, New Zealand, South Korea, UK, USA) to monitor sanctions outside UN framework.

**OFAC Actions:**
- July 8, 2025: Sanctioned Song Kum Hyok (Andariel-affiliated actor)
- August 27, 2025: Sanctioned Vitaliy Andreyev, Kim Ung Sun, Chinese front company, and money laundering entity

**DOJ Operations:**
Indicted 14 DPRK nationals generating over $88 million through IT worker schemes. Sought civil forfeiture of $7.7 million in crypto assets (June 2025).

## Recommended Defenses

Organizations should implement:
- Rigorous background checks with AI deepfake detection
- Multi-factor authentication across all access points
- Cold storage and multi-signature crypto wallets
- Endpoint detection and response (EDR) solutions
- SIEM systems for real-time anomaly detection
- Monitoring for unusual VPN usage and night-shift work patterns
- Regular employee training on phishing and social engineering

The report concludes that sustained vigilance, enhanced cyber resilience, and international cooperation remain critical to countering what it characterizes as a "sophisticated global criminal enterprise."
