## Overview

This document details the International Atomic Energy Agency's inspection and verification activities regarding Iraq's nuclear weapons program following UN Security Council Resolution 687 (1991).

## Key Points

**Initial Iraqi Obstruction**

Iraq failed to meet the resolution's timetable by pursuing "denial, concealment and obstruction" rather than providing required declarations. The IAEA discovered Iraq had hidden electromagnetic isotope separation equipment through burial and convoy movements between sites.

**Incomplete Declarations**

Iraq's April 1991 submission omitted critical facilities including uranium enrichment plants, weapons development centers, and gas centrifuge facilities. Only after UN intervention in July 1991 did Iraq provide expanded (though still incomplete) information.

**Major Discoveries**

The IAEA uncovered extensive weaponization research, plutonium recovery from reactor fuel reprocessing (approximately five grams), and Project 182 aimed at constructing a natural uranium/heavy water reactor for plutonium production.

**Documentation Cache**

Inspections in September-October 1991 yielded comprehensive documents proving Al Atheer's actual mission involved nuclear weapons development, despite Iraqi denials until mid-October.

**Destruction Campaign**

The IAEA destroyed over 50,000 square meters of facility space and 1,900 items, removing all known weapon-usable nuclear material by February 1994.

**Ongoing Concerns**

Despite finding "no indications" of weapons achievement, the document notes Iraq likely retained documentation, component specimens, and experienced personnel, creating inherent uncertainty in verification completeness.
