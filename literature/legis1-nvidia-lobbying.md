# NVIDIA Spends $1.9M Lobbying on Chip Controls, AI Policy

- **Source:** Legis1
- **Editor:** Joanne Levine
- **Published:** October 24, 2025
- **URL:** https://legis1.com/news/nvidia-semiconductor-export-lobbying/

---

## Why It Matters

NVIDIA confronts convergent regulatory pressures: Congressional movement to restrict semiconductor exports to China, intensifying antitrust review of its dominant position in AI processors, and evolving AI governance legislation.

The situation carries significant business implications. Legislative proposals including the No Advanced Chips for the CCP Act and the FIGHT China Act could directly constrain NVIDIA's export revenue. Senators Jim Banks and Elizabeth Warren have publicly pressured NVIDIA's leadership regarding circumventing export restrictions.

## By the Numbers

NVIDIA's third-quarter 2025 expenditure of $1.9 million marks a substantial increase—representing approximately 55 percent of its total 2025 in-house lobbying allocation of $3.46 million. Since 2015, the company has submitted 44 disclosures, with cumulative spending of $5.31 million.

The company recently brought aboard Flynn Rico-Johnson from Senator Amy Klobuchar's office, indicating preparation for heightened antitrust policy examination. Klobuchar has established herself as an influential technology competition policy advocate.

NVIDIA complements internal advocacy with external representation from firms including Tiber Creek Group Inc., Brownstein Hyatt Farber Schreck LLP, and BGR Government Affairs LLC.

The in-house team comprises five Capitol Hill-experienced lobbyists:

- **David Shahoulian:** 12.5 years of House experience, former House Judiciary Committee Subcommittee Chief Counsel; previously represented Intel on CHIPS Act execution
- **Flynn Rico-Johnson:** 8 years Senate/House background, Klobuchar staff member; initial NVIDIA work centers on AI and competition policy
- **Stewart Barber:** Previously lobbied for Intel on export controls and CHIPS Act matters
- **Zachary Noah Isakowitz:** Previously advocated for the Semiconductor Industry Association on supply chain security
- **Ned Arthur Finkle:** Represents NVIDIA on AI policy and semiconductor manufacturing

## The Agenda

NVIDIA is advocating on semiconductor export restrictions and AI governance, with specific focus on the Chip Security Act and the National Defense Authorization Act for FY 2026.

The company's advocacy concentrates on five domains: defense and semiconductor supply chain security, AI competition policy and workforce development, quantum computing and energy infrastructure, semiconductor design and fabrication regulation, and semiconductor trade and AI export controls.

## Broader Context

NVIDIA's lobbying acceleration reflects regulatory and geopolitical transformation in semiconductors and artificial intelligence. DeepSeek's reported circumvention of American export restrictions mobilized Congressional action to strengthen limitations on advanced chip transfers to China. The company also faces antitrust examination regarding its $100 billion OpenAI partnership.

American data centers are anticipated to require 426 terawatt-hours yearly by 2030, prompting Congressional concern regarding energy policy and data center oversight.

## Between The Lines

Significant pending legislation encompasses the No Advanced Chips for the CCP Act, prohibiting advanced AI semiconductor shipments to China, and the American Artificial Intelligence Leadership and Uniformity Act, establishing standardized national AI regulations.

Senator Tom Cotton communicated a public message that organizations circumventing export restrictions would encounter consequences. Conversely, Senators Mark Kelly and Ruben Gallego applauded NVIDIA's commitment to manufacturing AI supercomputers exclusively domestically.

## Competitive Landscape

NVIDIA's activity occurs alongside comparable efforts by industry competitors. The Information Technology Industry Council has advocated on AI and CHIPS Act funding. Intel Corporation has engaged substantially on CHIPS Act execution and export controls. The Semiconductor Industry Association has advocated for federal R&D investment and supply chain resilience.

## The Bottom Line

NVIDIA's $1.9 million third-quarter 2025 lobbying expenditure reflects intense Congressional pressure across multiple dimensions: China export restrictions, antitrust examination, data center energy considerations, and AI governance architecture.
